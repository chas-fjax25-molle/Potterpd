Discover the magical universe created by J.K. Rowling.

The Wizarding World of Harry Potter is a hidden magical society existing alongside the non-magical world. Founded on centuries of tradition, magic, and secrecy, it includes institutions like Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, the Ministry of Magic, and magical villages such as Hogsmeade. Wizards and witches live in secrecy to protect their world from Muggles, using spells, magical creatures, and enchanted objects in their daily lives.

The Boy Who Lived and his legendary journey.

Harry Potter is a young wizard famous for surviving an attack by the dark wizard Lord Voldemort as a baby. Raised by his non-magical relatives, Harry discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard and begins attending Hogwarts. Throughout seven years at school, he faces danger, friendship, loss, and destiny while uncovering the truth about his past and his connection to Voldemort.

The most prestigious school of witchcraft and wizardry.

Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry is located in Scotland and serves as the primary magical education institution in Britain. Students are sorted into four houses: Gryffindor, Slytherin, Ravenclaw, and Hufflepuff. They study subjects such as Potions, Transfiguration, Defense Against the Dark Arts, and Herbology. The castle itself is filled with moving staircases, enchanted ceilings, and hidden secrets.

The darkest wizard of modern times.

Born Tom Marvolo Riddle, Lord Voldemort sought immortality and ultimate power. He created Horcruxes—dark magical objects containing pieces of his soul—to avoid death. As the leader of the Death Eaters, he waged war against the wizarding world, promoting pure-blood supremacy. His conflict with Harry Potter forms the central storyline of the series.

Fantastic beasts and where they are found.

The Harry Potter universe features a vast range of magical creatures, from house-elves and hippogriffs to dragons and phoenixes. Some creatures are friendly and intelligent, while others are dangerous and unpredictable. The Ministry of Magic regulates magical creatures, classifying them according to threat level and magical ability.

A global phenomenon that shaped a generation.

The Harry Potter series consists of seven novels published between 1997 and 2007. The books became an international success, translated into dozens of languages and adapted into blockbuster films. Beyond entertainment, the series explores themes of friendship, courage, prejudice, and sacrifice. Today, Harry Potter remains one of the most influential literary franchises in modern history.
